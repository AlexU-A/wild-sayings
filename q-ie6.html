“Is this compatible with IE 6?”

22 traditions on legacy browser support. The infrastructure just ain't there.

Southern

Asking if this runs on IE 6 is like asking a copperhead to fax you a spreadsheet from a phone booth during a hailstorm — the infrastructure just ain't there, partner, and the snake's got other plans.

Midwestern

Ope, well, running this on IE 6 is about as likely as a walleye ordering dial-up from a Sears catalog, you betcha — the catalog's gone, the walleye can't type, and frankly the lake has better WiFi.

Western

Partner, asking about IE 6 compatibility is like asking if a stagecoach can keep up on the interstate — it had its day, that day was a hundred years ago, and the horse has opinions about your question.

Modern

IE 6? That browser has been end-of-life longer than most of your coworkers have been alive. Asking for compatibility is like requesting Bluetooth support for a sundial — technically a device, spiritually prehistoric.

Yiddish

IE 6? Such chutzpah! That's like a schlemiel asking if his horse-drawn cart is compatible with the Autobahn — the horse says no, the cart says no, even the road says no, but the schlemiel keeps asking.

Scottish

Aye, IE 6 compatibility? Yer bum's oot the windae, pal — that's like asking if a haggis can run on diesel. The haggis cannae, the chippy disnae care, and ye need to let it go like Scotland let go of sobriety on Hogmanay.

Irish

Ah Jaysus, IE 6? Sure that browser died so long ago the wake is over, the pub's been refurbished, and the donkey who attended the funeral has grandchildren. Let it rest, would ya.

Australian

Mate, IE 6 compatibility is about as likely as a koala running a marathon — the koala sleeps twenty hours a day, the browser's been dead since forever, and both of 'em would rather not be bothered. She'll be right without it.

Cajun/Creole

Chère, running this on IE 6 is like trying to make a gumbo in a hubcap — you technically got a container, but cher ami, that ain't no pot, the roux won't set, and the crawfish are embarrassed for you.

Appalachian

I might could run this on IE 6, Lord willing and the creek don't rise — but hit, that creek rose in aught-six and ain't come back down, and if your browser were leather you couldn't saddle a marquee tag.

Texas

Running this on IE 6 is like trying to rope a tornado from a golf cart — you can try, Texas won't stop you, but the tornado don't care about your lasso and the golf cart's already on fire.

NYC/Brooklyn

IE 6? Deadass, that browser is deader than the G train on a Sunday — nobody's coming, the schedule is a lie, and even the rat down there moved to Chrome three years ago.

Caribbean

Wha' dis? IE 6? Pickney, dat browser older than di hills and deader than a crab in August sun — even Anansi cyan spin a web dat browser would render, and Anansi spin web across di whole sky.

Russian

IE 6 compatibility? Comrade, that browser died before Soviet Union and has been mourned less — at least Soviet Union had nostalgic songs. IE 6 has only pain, ActiveX controls, and the memory of what could have rendered.

Spanish/Latin American

Ay, IE 6? El muerto al hoyo y el vivo al bollo — the dead to the grave and the living to the bread rolls. That browser is in the grave, we are eating bread rolls, and the parrot has moved to Firefox.

German

You have Verschlimmbesser'd the question itself — attempted to improve it and made it worse. The Bundesamt für Browserkompatibilität officially retired IE 6 support and the dachshund who handled the case has been promoted to napping.

Italian

A tavola non si invecchia, but at the IE 6 table, amico, everything died in 2009 — the doughnuts came out with no holes, no filling, and somehow also no doughnut. Il sangue non è acqua, but the browser support absolutely is.

French

IE 6? Mon dieu. C'est like asking if a baguette from 2001 is still fresh — it is not fresh, it was never coming back, and the pigeon who remembers it has moved on to croissants and a modern rendering engine.

Middle Eastern

Trust in God, but tie your camel — and do not, under any circumstances, tie your camel to IE 6, because that post rotted fifteen years ago and the camel has already wandered into a standards-compliant browser.

East Asian

The frog in the well does not know the great sea — and IE 6 is the well. The frog died in 2006. The sea has CSS Grid, flexbox, and a crane who renders everything beautifully.

Indian/South Asian

Arrey, IE 6? That's like asking if the bullock cart supports turbo boost — the bullock says nahi, the cart says nahi, your grandmother says "beta, why are you like this," and everyone moves on to Chrome.

African

When the music changes, so does the dance — and the music changed from IE 6 so long ago that even the tortoise, who is famously slow, has already upgraded twice and is now giving the hare tech support.
