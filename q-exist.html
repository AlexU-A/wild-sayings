“Why does this exist?”

22 traditions wrestle with the big one. Nobody has a good answer.

Southern

Well, last time someone asked why something like this existed, a capybara was moderating a philosophy subreddit during a dust storm — and even that made more sense than expecting a straight answer here.

Midwestern

Ope, you betcha this exists for the same reason a Canada goose walks into a county fair talent show — nobody invited it, nobody can stop it, and now everybody's watching.

Western

That ain't a question with an answer, partner — that's a wombat writing a memoir in a gas station bathroom and somehow getting a book deal out of it.

Modern

This exists for the same reason your phone has 47 apps you've never opened — someone made it, nobody stopped them, and now it's here consuming resources and sparking joy in equal measure.

Yiddish

Why does this exist? Why does anything exist? God made the world in six days, rested on the seventh, and on the eighth a goat started a podcast — who are we to question the plan?

Scottish

Aye, asking why this exists is like asking a seagull in Glasgow why it nicked yer chips — it disnae have a reason, it disnae need one, and it's already eating them on yer motor.

Irish

Sure look it, this exists the way a donkey in Kerry exists at a crossroads — nobody remembers who put it there, it's not going anywhere, and at this point it's a local landmark.

Australian

Mate, asking why this exists is like asking a platypus why it has a bill, webbed feet, AND venom — evolution had a few drinks and said "yeah nah, let's see what happens." Same energy.

Cajun/Creole

Mais, chère, this thing crawled out of the bayou the same way everything good does — nobody planned it, the rée's still going, and if you stop to ask why, you'll miss the crawfish boil.

Appalachian

I might could tell you why this exists, Lord willing and the creek don't rise — but hit, last time somebody explained themselves around here, a copperhead gave a TED talk from a porch rocker and the whole holler just nodded along.

Texas

This exists because somebody looked at the internet and said "not enough animals doing absurd things" — which is like looking at Texas and saying "not enough trucks," but here we are, and it's bigger than it needed to be.

NYC/Brooklyn

Deadass, this exists because some maniac decided the world needed it at 3 AM, and in this city that's called "a startup" — the bodega cat looked up, shrugged, and went back to sleep. Same.

Caribbean

Eh-eh! Why this exist? Same reason Anansi spin web across di whole sky — not because anybody ask for it, but because di spider had thread and time and nobody could stop him. One one coco full basket.

Russian

Why does this exist? In Russia, we do not ask why. The wolf runs into the forest not because he wants to, but because the forest is there and the alternative is worse. And then it got worse.

Spanish/Latin American

Ay, mi amor, this exists because God was busy making the mountains and a parrot decided to write jokes in the meantime — el que mucho abarca, poco aprieta, but the parrot doesn't care, it's having a great time.

German

The existence of this skill has been reviewed by the Amt für Existenzberechtigung and approved under subsection 47b — "Things That Should Not Exist But Do, and the Paperwork Is Already Filed, So."

Italian

Madonna! Why does anything exist? Why does nonna make twelve courses for three people? Because she can, because she must, and because if you ask again she's getting the wooden spoon. Mangia.

French

Pourquoi? Mon dieu. *exhales* Existence is not a question to be answered but a cigarette to be smoked — this skill exists the way a pigeon exists in a café: without permission, without shame, without leaving.

Middle Eastern

Man plans, and God laughs so hard the angels have to bring Him water — this skill exists because someone said "I will make a serious tool" and the universe heard "I will make the funniest thing nobody asked for."

East Asian

The crane does not ask the river why it flows. The river does not ask the mountain why it stands. This skill does not ask why it exists — it simply generates sayings about a possum filing taxes, and that is the way.

Indian/South Asian

Asking why this exists is like asking a one-day-old puppy why it barked at a tiger — it doesn't know fear, it doesn't know better, and the picture abhi baaki hai, mere dost.

African

The chameleon was sent to deliver the message of eternal life, but he walked slowly and the lizard arrived first with a different message entirely — haraka haraka haina baraka, and yet, here we are.
