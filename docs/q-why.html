“Why god why?”

22 traditions stare into the abyss. The abyss sends back a saying.

Southern

Well honey, last time somebody asked the Almighty a question like that, a possum in a choir robe was fixin' to baptize a catfish in a Waffle House parking lot during a thunderstorm, and the Lord just shrugged and refilled His sweet tea.

Midwestern

Oh jeez, well, the last time someone asked "why" up here, a walleye looked up from Lake Superior, made eye contact, and both parties agreed to just let it go and have some hotdish instead. Some things don't need answers.

Western

Partner, asking "why" out here is like asking a vulture why it circles — it just does. The canyon don't explain itself, the dust don't apologize, and the mule at the bottom ain't looking for meaning, just water.

Modern

"Why" is just the loading screen of the human experience — it spins forever, never resolves, and eventually you just close the tab and watch a raccoon open a trash can on YouTube like the rest of us.

Yiddish

Why god why? Such a question! God's been getting this one since Abraham, and even He just pinches the bridge of His nose and says "oy, this one again." The goat next to you in line has been waiting longer. Sit. Eat.

Scottish

Och, "why god why" — aye, that's what the midges ask every winter when the cold comes and they cannae bite anyone. God disnae answer the midges either. Have a whisky and get on with it, pal.

Irish

Ah Jaysus, "why god why" — that's the national prayer, so it is. A sheep in Kerry asked the same thing during a storm and God sent more rain, which is His way of saying "I heard you, now have a pint."

Australian

Why? Mate, that's like asking a platypus why it exists — half beaver, half duck, full of venom, makes no sense, and yet here it is, thriving. Stop asking why and just be the platypus. She'll be right.

Cajun/Creole

Mais, chère, "why" down here is just the sound you make before the second line starts — you say it, the trombone answers, the nutria starts dancing on the neutral ground, and suddenly the question don't matter no more.

Appalachian

Lord have mercy, "why" is what the holler asks the mountain every morning and the mountain just sits there — it don't owe you an answer, same as the copperhead don't owe you a warning. Some things just are.

Texas

Why? In Texas, we don't ask why — we ask "how big." The longhorn doesn't question the fence, it just decides whether to jump it or walk through it, and either way, it's the fence's problem now.

NYC/Brooklyn

Why god why? Deadass, the rat on the G train has been asking the same thing since 2003 and he's still here, still grinding, still dragging a full slice down the stairs. God ain't answering. The rat adapted.

Caribbean

Why? Pickney, Anansi asked God di same question and God turned him into a spider — now Anansi spin stories instead of asking questions, and everybody happier for it. One one coco full basket.

Russian

You ask why? In Russia, we do not ask why. We simply watch as bear puts on necktie, files for mortgage, and accepts that the queue for answers stretches past horizon and office closed in 1987. Nichevo.

Spanish/Latin American

Ay, why? Dios aprieta pero no ahorca — God squeezes but doesn't strangle. The parrot screams "why" every morning and the donkey just keeps walking. The sun comes up either way and the tortillas won't make themselves.

German

The question "warum" has been submitted to the Existenzamt and processed through seventeen sub-departments. The official response, stamped by a very tired Dackel: "Weil." Because. The office is now closed.

Italian

Madonna mia, why? WHY? That's like asking nonna why she made enough pasta for a village when three people are coming — she throws her hands up, the espresso boils over, the cat knocks a saint off the windowsill, and somehow this is YOUR fault.

French

Pourquoi? *lights cigarette* The pigeon does not ask why it exists in the café. The café does not ask why it exists on the boulevard. We are all just smoke and espresso, chéri. Have another croissant and stop asking.

Middle Eastern

Man plans, and God laughs so hard the angels have to bring Him water — and when man asks "why," God laughs again, and the camel who was eavesdropping just keeps chewing because the camel learned long ago not to ask.

East Asian

The student asks the master "why?" The master pours tea until it overflows. "Like this cup," says the master, "you are too full of questions." The crane outside has never asked why and is doing great.

Indian/South Asian

Arrey bhai, "why god why?" — even Lord Ganesha, remover of obstacles, looked at this question and said "beta, some obstacles are load-bearing." The monkey god is laughing. Your auntie is making chai. Let it go.

African

The chameleon walks slowly because it has already seen what is ahead — and what is ahead is the same "why" that every creature has asked since the first drumbeat. The answer was always in the rhythm, not the words.
