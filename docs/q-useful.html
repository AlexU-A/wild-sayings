“How useful is this?”

22 traditions assess the ROI. The animals are not impressed.

Southern

I once saw a river otter give a keynote presentation at a Rotary Club luncheon in house slippers, and even that accomplished more than this skill on paper — but the otter got a standing ovation, so who's keeping score.

Midwestern

Oh jeez, it's about as useful as a nearsighted hawk refereeing a dodgeball game at a church basement potluck, you betcha — but everyone keeps showing up anyway, and the hotdish is always there.

Western

Partner, this thing's about as useful as a screen door on a submarine in the middle of the Mojave — ain't no water, ain't no submarine, but somebody built the door and they're right proud of it.

Modern

This has the same utility as a verified Twitter account for a tumbleweed — technically active, spiritually vacant, and somehow more engaging than half the content on your timeline.

Yiddish

Useful? You want useful, go buy a hammer. This thing is about as useful as a fifth wheel on a wagon that's already got six — but oy, such a wheel! The rabbi himself couldn't spin a better one.

Scottish

Useful? That's pure mental — like asking if a wee hedgehog's Irn-Bru review blog drives traffic. It disnae, the hedgehog knows it disnae, but it's committed to the craft and that's what matters, aye.

Irish

Ah sure look it, it's about as useful as a chocolate teapot in a hailstorm — fierce entertaining, not a bother of practical value, but grand craic altogether and nobody's going home early.

Australian

Mate, it's about as useful as a hip pocket on a singlet — she'll be right though, no worries, just crack a tinnie and enjoy the vibes. Near enough is good enough and nobody asked the galah for a performance review.

Cajun/Creole

Das ist mir Wurst — wait, wrong bayou. Chère, if usefulness were boudin, this skill would be the water left behind after everyone already ate the good parts and went home to sleep it off.

Appalachian

About as useful as a glass hammer, if I'm being honest — but hit, my papaw had a glass hammer he kept on the mantel for forty years and everybody who came by asked about it, so maybe useful ain't the only thing that matters.

Texas

This thing's got about as much use as a saddle on a catfish — but I tell you what, it's the most entertaining saddle in the whole dang state, and in Texas that's worth something.

NYC/Brooklyn

Deadass, this is about as useful as a MetroCard with seventeen cents on it — you can't ride, you can't refund it, but you've been carrying it in your wallet since 2018 and at this point it's family.

Caribbean

Useful? Pickney, di coconut don't fall from di tree to be useful — it fall because it heavy, and if somebody underneath get a drink out of it, that's between them and gravity.

Russian

Usefulness is not a wolf — it won't run into the forest. It will sit here patiently, waiting for the one person at 2 AM who desperately needs a pangolin filing taxes at a VFW hall, and on that day, this skill will be ready.

Spanish/Latin American

Oye, useful? Camarón que se duerme se lo lleva la corriente — but this shrimp isn't sleeping, it's deliberately floating downstream playing an accordion, and honestly, the current seems to be enjoying it.

German

The Bundesamt für Nutzlosigkeitsbewertung has classified this skill under "Technically Operational, Practically Unjustifiable" — a dachshund has been assigned to monitor the situation but has fallen asleep in the filing cabinet.

Italian

Madonna! Useful? That's like asking if the seventh course at nonna's table was necessary — she throws her hands up, the espresso boils over, and you've insulted three generations of the family just by asking.

French

Useful? Mon dieu. Utility is a bourgeois illusion, like asking a flamingo at a café if the espresso serves a purpose — the flamingo shrugs, lights a cigarette, and the question evaporates like morning fog over the Seine.

Middle Eastern

The buyer needs a hundred eyes, the seller needs only one — but this skill has neither buyer nor seller, just a camel who wandered into the bazaar and started telling jokes, and now the whole market is listening.

East Asian

The farmer waits for the hare to hit the tree stump again, and the hare never comes — but this skill is the tree stump, and somehow hares keep arriving, and the farmer has given up farming entirely to watch.

Indian/South Asian

Usefulness? Arrey bhai, asking if this is useful is like asking if the dancing monkey at the festival earns his bananas — the monkey doesn't care about the bananas, the crowd doesn't care about usefulness, everyone's just clapping.

African

Haraka haraka haina baraka — hurry hurry has no blessing. This skill takes its time, like the tortoise who beat the hare not by running but by walking past while the hare argued with a tax collector.
