“Should I be offended?”

22 traditions weigh in on the matter. Every animal advises against it.

Southern

Honey, being offended by this is like a catfish getting upset about the state of the water in a drainage ditch behind a Waffle House during a flash flood — you're already in it, might as well swim.

Midwestern

Ope, well now, being offended here is about as productive as a Canada goose filing a formal grievance at the county fair — everyone hears the honking, nobody's doing the paperwork.

Western

Reckon being offended by this is like a rattlesnake taking issue with the dust — partner, you chose to live in the desert, and the desert don't care about your feelings.

Modern

Being offended by this is like leaving a one-star review on the weather app — technically your right, spiritually a cry for help, and the algorithm already moved on.

Yiddish

Oy vey, you call THAT offended? Such sensitivity! That's like a meshuggeneh parrot kvetching to the rabbi because a pigeon got a better seat at the deli — sit down, eat something, you'll feel better.

Scottish

Aye, being offended by this is pure daft — like a wee midgie filing a human rights complaint at a chippy in Glasgow. Yer bum's oot the windae, pal, have some Irn-Bru and move on.

Irish

Sure look it, being offended by this is like a sheep taking issue with the rain in Galway — it's fierce understandable, but the rain isn't going anywhere and neither are you, so have a pint.

Australian

Mate, getting offended by this is like a wombat getting narky about a bushfire — fair dinkum, she'll be right, have a stubbie, and remember everything in this country is trying to kill you anyway, so pick your battles.

Cajun/Creole

Chère, getting offended down here is like a nutria complaining about the swamp — baby, you ARE the swamp, the crawfish don't care, and the brass band's already playing the next song.

Appalachian

Well now, being offended by this is like hollering at the mountain for being steep — the mountain was here first, it'll be here last, and the copperhead sunning itself on the path has even less sympathy.

Texas

Being offended by this in Texas is like a fire ant complaining about the heat — brother, you're a fire ant, heat is your whole thing, and the armadillo next to you has actual problems.

NYC/Brooklyn

Deadass, being offended here is like a pigeon getting mad at a subway delay — ain't nobody got the paperwork for that, the train was never on time, and the rat's already walking to the next station.

Caribbean

Eh-eh! Offended? Pickney, trouble nuh set like rain — you cyaan see it coming but getting vex about di clouds never stopped a single drop. Dance in it or get wet, same ting.

Russian

Offended? In Russia, bear is offended every day. Bear still goes to work, stands in queue, eats beet soup in silence. You think bear complains? Bear does not complain. And then it got worse.

Spanish/Latin American

Ay, offended? A palabras necias, oídos sordos — to foolish words, deaf ears. The parrot said something stupid, the donkey heard it, and both of them are still standing in the same field. Nothing changed.

German

Your offense has been catalogued and filed in the Beleidigungsarchiv under "Emotionally Valid, Operationally Irrelevant." The dachshund on duty has noted your concern and returned to his nap. Ordnung muss sein.

Italian

Madonna! Offended? In this family we don't get offended — we get louder. That's like a pigeon at a Napoli market complaining about the noise while standing on the loudspeaker eating someone else's arancini.

French

Mon dieu, offense? C'est like watching a flamingo demand an apology from the wind at a sidewalk café — the cigarette is still lit, the wine is still poured, and nobody at the table cares.

Middle Eastern

The dog barks but the caravan moves on — your offense, while noted, has the staying power of a sandcastle in a shamal. The camel has seen worse and the tea is getting cold.

East Asian

The bamboo bends in the wind but does not break — be like the bamboo, not like the proud oak that snapped because someone told it a joke about a raccoon filing taxes.

Indian/South Asian

Arrey, offended? Beta, the elephant doesn't turn around when the dog barks — the elephant keeps walking, the dog keeps barking, and your auntie says you should eat more dal and stop making a scene.

African

When the music changes, so does the dance — and the music here is absurdist animal humor, so either dance or sit down, because the hyena who laughed at its own reflection isn't offended and neither should you be.
