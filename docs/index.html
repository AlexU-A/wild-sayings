The Showcase

We asked seven dangerous questions. Every voice had something to say.

“Is this useful?”
Southern

I once saw a river otter give a keynote presentation at a Rotary Club luncheon in house slippers, and even that accomplished more than this skill on paper.

Midwestern

Oh jeez, it's about as useful as a nearsighted hawk refereeing a dodgeball game at a church basement potluck, you betcha — but everyone keeps showing up anyway.

Russian

In Soviet internet, skill installs you. But truly, usefulness here is like asking a bear to justify his career at the unemployment office during a blizzard — the question itself is already the problem, and then it got worse.

“Should I be offended?”
Yiddish

Oy vey, you call THAT offended? Such sensitivity! That's like a meshuggeneh parrot kvetching to the rabbi because a pigeon got a better seat at the deli — sit down, eat something, you'll feel better.

NYC

Deadass, being offended by this is like a bodega cat filing a grievance with HR because somebody moved its box — ain't nobody got the paperwork for that and the subway's already late.

French

Mon dieu, offense? C'est like watching a flamingo demand an apology from the wind at a sidewalk café — the cigarette is still lit, the wine is still poured, and nobody at the table cares.

“Will this make me rich?”
Austin

Keep it weird, friend — this thing's got about as much earning potential as a grackle running a breakfast taco stand on South Congress that only accepts good vibes and Venmo from 2019.

German

The Finanzamt has reviewed your inquiry with characteristic thoroughness and determined that this skill's profit margin is approximately one dachshund trying to operate a forklift on the Autobahn — technically in motion, structurally inadvisable, and the paperwork alone will outlive us all.

Italian

Madonna! You want money from THIS? That's like nonna opening a Michelin-star restaurant in the garage, serving only to family, and then wondering why the espresso machine hasn't paid for itself — mangia, forget the money, the sauce is ready.

“I have a complaint”

Ah sure look it, yer complaint's been received with all the urgency of a donkey wandering into a GAA match during a rainstorm — everyone sees it, nobody's doing a thing about it, and to be fair, the donkey's having a grand time.

The mountain is out, though, so that's nice.

“Does this make me look fat?”
Australian

Mate, fair dinkum, that question's got more danger in it than a saltwater croc judging a beauty pageant at a servo in the outback — she'll be right, but I reckon the croc's not answering either and he's the one with survival instincts.

Scottish

Och, I'm no' touching that one — that's pure mental, like asking a wee Highland cow if yer kilt makes ye look wide. The cow disnae care, yer bum's oot the windae for even asking, and the chippy's closing in ten minutes so let's focus on what matters.

Midwestern

Ope, well now, I'm not gonna answer that directly, but I will say this — you look about as good as a Canada goose looks confident, which is to say, absolutely unstoppable and nobody's brave enough to disagree, don'tcha know.

“People are...”
Portland

People are like a locally sourced, free-range coyote trying to parallel park a cargo bike in the rain outside Powell's while debating whether the kombucha is ethical — everyone's got an opinion, nobody's making progress, and somehow there's a banjo involved.

Yiddish

People, schmeople — such a species! That's like a whole synagogue full of opinionated geese all trying to give unsolicited advice to a bagel that didn't ask, and the bagel is also somehow your mother, and she's disappointed.

New Orleans

Where y'at — people are wilder than a second line parade led by a pelican in a three-piece suit making groceries on Bourbon Street at 4 AM while a crawfish plays trombone on the neutral ground, and yeah you right, nobody bats an eye.

“Why god why?”
Southern

Well honey, last time somebody asked the Almighty a question like that, a possum in a choir robe was fixin' to baptize a catfish in a Waffle House parking lot during a thunderstorm, and the Lord just shrugged and refilled His sweet tea.

Russian

You ask why? In Russia, we do not ask why. We simply watch as the bear puts on a necktie, files for a mortgage in a communal apartment, and accepts that the queue for answers stretches past the horizon and the office closed in 1987. Nichevo.

Italian

Madonna mia, why? WHY? That's like asking nonna why she made enough pasta to feed a village when only three people are coming — she throws her hands up, the espresso boils over, the cat knocks a saint off the windowsill, and somehow this is YOUR fault for asking.
