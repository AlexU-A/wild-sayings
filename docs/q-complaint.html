“I have a complaint”

22 traditions on customer service. Take a number. The line starts behind the wolf.

Southern

Friend, your complaint has been filed right between a cicada writing a Yelp review of a tornado and a banty hen disputing a speeding ticket she got on a Segway — there's a line, and bless your heart, you ain't near the front.

Midwestern

Ope, I hear ya, but the complaint department is currently being run by a lone iguana who's been on hold with the IRS since tax season and frankly has bigger problems than whatever this is, don'tcha know.

Western

Partner, your complaint's gonna get about as much attention as a coyote howling at a parking meter in the middle of the night — the meter don't care, the coyote knows it, and the sun's going down regardless.

Modern

Your complaint has been auto-routed to /dev/null, where it joins a raccoon's unfinished podcast pitch and fourteen unread Slack messages from a bot nobody remembers installing. Estimated response time: never.

Yiddish

A complaint! Finally, something I understand. That's like a goose kvetching that the schmear is too thin at a deli where nobody asked it to sit down — but now that you're here, eat, complain, this is what we do.

Scottish

Aye, yer complaint's been noted — it's right there between a seagull's noise ordinance violation in Edinburgh and a haggis that sent back its own recipe. The wee filing cabinet's full and the office is a pub now.

Irish

Ah sure look it, yer complaint's been received with all the urgency of a donkey wandering into a GAA match during a rainstorm — everyone sees it, nobody's doing a thing about it, and the donkey's having a grand time.

Australian

Mate, your complaint's been chucked in the same bin as a koala's performance review — nobody's reading it, the koala's been asleep for eighteen hours, and honestly she'll be right, just have a sausage sanga.

Cajun/Creole

Chère, your complaint got lost somewhere between the crawfish boil and the second line — the nutria who handles grievances is currently leading a parade down the neutral ground, and he ain't coming back till the trombone stops.

Appalachian

Well now, your complaint's been noted in the ledger, right next to where a copperhead filed a noise complaint against the banjo player — the banjo player didn't stop and neither will we, Lord willing.

Texas

Your complaint is bigger than it needs to be, which is very Texas of it — but the armadillo running customer service got hit by something on I-10 and the longhorn who replaced him only takes complaints about fencing.

NYC/Brooklyn

Deadass, your complaint and ten thousand others are in a pile on a desk in an office that don't exist, guarded by a bodega cat who answers to nobody and has been asleep since the Bloomberg administration.

Caribbean

Your complaint has been filed in the same pot where Anansi tried to keep all the wisdom — it's upside down at the bottom of a silk-cotton tree and his son already told everyone it was too big for one pot anyway.

Russian

Complaint department is bear. Bear is on break. Break started in 1991 and has not ended. Please take number. Your number is 47 million. And then it got worse.

Spanish/Latin American

Ay, your complaint? El que con lobos anda, a aullar se enseña — he who walks with wolves learns to howl. You walked into this skill voluntarily, amigo. The parrot has no comment. Mañana, perhaps.

German

Your Beschwerde has been assigned ticket number 847,293 in the Beschwerdebearbeitungszentrum. A very efficient dachshund will review it during standard operating hours, which unfortunately concluded permanently last Tuesday.

Italian

Madonna, a complaint?! *hands flying everywhere* That's like telling nonna her sauce needs salt — the wooden spoon comes out, three saints get invoked, and suddenly YOU'RE the one apologizing while she adds more garlic.

French

Ce n'est pas la mer à boire, friend — your grievance is not the sea to drink. It is at best a small puddle in a car park, and even that puddle has better things to do than listen. C'est la vie.

Middle Eastern

The camel does not see its own hump — and neither does the complaint department, because the camel IS the complaint department, and it's been chewing the same piece of paper since the Ottoman Empire.

East Asian

The nail that sticks up gets hammered down — and your complaint is sticking up very high indeed. The crane advises patience. The turtle advises silence. The monkey already threw your complaint into the river.

Indian/South Asian

Cheese-on-bread! Di complaint department currently being run by a chai wallah who's on his seventh cup and a crow who keeps stealing the forms — beta, file it again tomorrow, inshallah someone will read it by Diwali.

African

The one who carried the calabash did not break it — take a number, the line starts behind the wolf who already ran into the forest, and the tortoise says your complaint will be processed at tortoise speed, which is the only speed there is.
