# Wild Sayings — Dialect Reference

21 regional/cultural humor traditions with structural patterns, vocabulary, and example sayings. Referenced by SKILL.md for the generator's regional flavor rotation.

---

## 1. Southern (USA)

**Comedy DNA:** Theatrical delivery, escalating imagery, animal chaos, layered heat sources.
**Vocabulary:** y'all, fixin' to, bless your heart, reckon, ain't got the sense God gave a...
**Settings:** Waffle House, front porch, church parking lot, the holler, the creek
**Props:** sweet tea, screen doors, rocking chairs, pickup trucks, cast iron

**Key Patterns:**
- **Vulnerability trap:** "nervous as a long-tailed cat in a room full of rocking chairs"
- **Wrong-thing-wrong-place:** "useless as a screen door on a submarine"
- **Conditional reductio:** "if his brains were leather, he couldn't saddle a June bug"
- **Inanimate causality:** "so ugly she'd make a freight train take a dirt road"
- **False benediction:** "Bless your heart" + devastating follow-up

## 2. Midwestern (USA)

**Comedy DNA:** Understatement, passive-aggressive politeness, cheerful fatalism, weaponized manners.
**Vocabulary:** ope, oh jeez, oh for Pete's sake, oh for cute, you betcha, don'tcha know, I suppose, that's... different, hey? (terminal)
**Settings:** church basement, potluck table, county fair, Casey's General Store, Kwik Trip, VFW hall, Lutheran fellowship hour
**Props:** hotdish, Jell-O salad, cream of mushroom soup, slow cooker, folding tables, John Deere

**Key Patterns:**
- **Fatal pause:** "That's... different." (the pause converts it to disapproval)
- **Weaponized manners:** "Well, isn't that just... nice."
- **Domestic-as-cosmic-scale:** "That's like bringing store-bought to a church potluck."
- **Ope reflex:** "Ope, sorry — just gonna sneak right past ya."
- **Terminal dangler:** "I wouldn't have done it that way, but."

## 3. Western / Cowboy (USA)

**Comedy DNA:** Deadpan delivery, laconic compression, practical metaphor, precision over randomness.
**Vocabulary:** partner, reckon, I'll be, that dog won't hunt, afoot or on horseback
**Settings:** dusty ranch, rodeo, the corral, the bunkhouse, a fence line, a two-lane highway
**Props:** barbed wire, saddles, spurs, a two-dollar steak, frog hair

**Key Patterns:**
- **Substance gap:** "all hat and no cattle"
- **Three-rule pivot:** "never approach a bull from the front, a horse from the rear, or a fool from any direction"
- **Laconic aphorism:** "Never miss a good chance to shut up."
- **Impossible-fine:** "finer than frog hair split four ways"
- **Price signals quality:** "hotter than a two-dollar pistol on the Fourth of July"

## 4. Modern / Universal

**Comedy DNA:** Deadpan over drawl, bureaucratic register as irony, self as punchline, algorithms as characters.
**Vocabulary:** giving, same energy, iconic, bold of you, main character, buffering, 404, doom-scrolling, side hustle, blessed
**Settings:** Slack channel, subway, co-working space, DoorDash delivery zone, Zoom call, group chat
**Props:** browser tabs, streaming queues, push notifications, LinkedIn profiles, AirPods, ring lights

**Key Patterns:**
- **Tech state mapping:** "Has the energy of a 404 page — technically present, completely useless"
- **Bold-of-you snowclone:** "Bold of you to assume I have a five-year plan."
- **Hustle brag inversion:** "Woke up at 4AM, stared at the ceiling, called it strategic thinking. Blessed."
- **Nature doc urban creature:** "That rat wasn't fleeing. That rat was relocating."
- **Translation format:** "I said 'sounds good' but what I meant was 'I will think about this until I die.'"

## 5. Yiddish

**Comedy DNA:** Fatalism as punchline, God as passive audience, money and suffering always connected, the advice is technically correct.
**Vocabulary:** oy vey, nebekh, schlemiel, nu, mazel, from your mouth to God's ears
**Settings:** the kitchen table, the old country, the doctor's office, anywhere money changes hands
**Props:** pillows, tight shoes, horseradish, chopped liver

**Key Patterns:**
- **Cosmic contrast:** "Man plans, God laughs." (A mensh trakht un Got lakht)
- **Wish-fulfillment-as-curse:** "May you have a hundred rooms and be found dead in every one."
- **Cure worse than disease:** "If you want to forget your troubles, put on shoes that are too tight."
- **Market logic + mortality:** "If the rich could hire others to die for them, the poor would make a very good living."
- **Impossible imperative:** "Sleep faster — we need the pillows."

## 6. Scottish

**Comedy DNA:** Compression over escalation, pessimism worn as pride, the "wee" trick (understatement that means the opposite).
**Vocabulary:** och, aye, dinnae, yer, awa' wi' ye, dreich, glaikit, wee (ironically), lang may yer lum reek
**Settings:** the pub, the North Sea coast, the football pitch, the chippy, a cold stone kirk
**Props:** whisky (no e), haggis, the haar (sea mist), a good wool jumper

**Key Patterns:**
- **Anatomical flat dismissal:** "Yer bum's oot the windae" (you're talking nonsense)
- **Imperative absurd self-harm:** "Away an bile yer heid" (go boil your head)
- **Inverted fatalism:** "Dinnae like the weather? Wait five minutes — it'll get worse."
- **Wee-word softening:** "a wee hauf an' a wee hauf-an'" (a "small" whisky + a "small" beer chaser)
- **Presbyterian resignation:** "Whit's fur ye'll no go past ye." (what's meant for you won't pass you by)

## 7. Irish

**Comedy DNA:** Indirection over escalation, the postscript sting, fatalism as warm comedy, weaponized blessings.
**Vocabulary:** sure, grand, craic, eejit, gobshite, yer man, God love him/her, it's a soft day
**Settings:** the pub, a rain-soaked field, the church gate, a stone wall, the kitchen table
**Props:** the kettle, a pint of Guinness, a good coat, the holy water

**Key Patterns:**
- **Limping curse:** "May God turn their ankles, so we'll know them by their limping."
- **Paradox blessing:** "May misfortune follow you all your days and never catch up."
- **God love them:** "The lights are on but nobody home — God love him." (insult + compassionate postscript)
- **Physics override:** "He's so contrary, if you threw him in the river he'd float upstream."
- **It'll be grand:** "Sure, it'll be grand." (crisis dismissed with vague future confidence)
- **Cold window:** "God never closes a door without opening a window — sometimes it's just to let in the cold."

## 8. Australian

**Comedy DNA:** Crude understatement as virtue, deadly wildlife as casual reference, pub social code as moral framework.
**Vocabulary:** mate, she'll be right, no worries, crikey, fair dinkum, drongo, galah, chook, grog, shout (buy a round)
**Settings:** the outback, the shearing shed, the pub, the beach, a ute, Bunnings car park
**Props:** a singlet, thongs (flip-flops), a stubby holder, a ute, Vegemite

**Key Patterns:**
- **Deadly animal comparison:** "Mad as a cut snake"
- **Outback heat hyperbole:** "Hotter than a shearer's armpit" / "Dry as a dead dingo's donger"
- **Incompetence assessment:** "Couldn't organise a chook raffle in a pub"
- **Meanness test:** "Wouldn't shout if a shark bit him"
- **Pure understatement:** "She'll be right" / "Near enough is good enough"
- **Paddock character:** "A few kangaroos loose in the top paddock"

## 9. Cajun / Louisiana Creole

**Comedy DNA:** Bayou survival wisdom as social caution, food as moral currency, French-Catholic joie de vivre as defiance.
**Vocabulary:** cher, mais, tonnerre mes chiens!, lagniappe, lâche pas la patate, laissez les bons temps rouler, boucherie
**Settings:** the bayou, the boucherie, the fais do-do, the pirogue, a roux-dark kitchen
**Props:** boudin, andouille, gumbo, crawfish, the pirogue, a cast-iron pot

**Key Patterns:**
- **Bayou crossing warning:** "Don't call the alligator big-mouth till you've crossed the river."
- **Transformation-to-worthlessness:** "Turn into boudin water" (become the thing discarded after value is extracted)
- **Easy-job accusation:** "He's the one who unhangs the andouilles." (shows up only for the reward)
- **Tidal resignation:** "La baie a couru sa course." (the bay has run its race — it's over)
- **Revenge escalation:** "The syrup and the biscuits don't break even." (trying to even the score only makes it worse)

## 10. Appalachian (USA)

**Comedy DNA:** Archaic English preserved in mountains, poverty as dignity, precise animal imagery from working-animal culture.
**Vocabulary:** hit (for "it"), nary, might could, directly (meaning soon), right smart, come up (for weather), fair up
**Settings:** the holler, the creek, the front porch, the root cellar, the church, a mountain trail
**Props:** a gourd, a mule, briars, a rocking chair, a quilt, moonshine

**Key Patterns:**
- **Mountain animal precision:** "Grinning like a mule eating briars through a picket fence"
- **Named micro-seasons:** "Blackberry winter" / "Dogwood winter" (cold snaps named by what's blooming)
- **Archaic conditional:** "If his brains were leather, he couldn't saddle a June bug"
- **Double modal:** "I might could do that" (graduated certainty as politeness)
- **Creek theology:** "Lord willing and the creek don't rise" (God's will and creek level equally weighted)
- **Dignified poverty:** "We were poor, but we didn't know it" / "Make do or do without"

## 11. Texas (USA)

**Comedy DNA:** Everything-is-bigger scale, Republic-era sovereignty, Tex-Mex cultural fusion, direct confrontation where Southern hides behind politeness.
**Vocabulary:** fixin' to, y'all, all hat and no cattle, that dog'll hunt, bless your heart (said directly)
**Settings:** the ranch, the rodeo, Marfa, the Permian Basin, a BBQ joint, the state capitol
**Props:** cowboy boots, a Stetson, brisket, tamales, a pickup truck, oil derricks

**Key Patterns:**
- **Scale obsession:** "Texas is the only place where you can look farther and see less."
- **Tex-Mex heat:** "Hotter than a stolen tamale" (double meaning: temperature + stolen goods)
- **Nature personification:** "The trees are bribing the dogs." (drought so bad trees beg for moisture)
- **Identity transformation denial:** "You can put your boots in the oven but that don't make 'em biscuits."
- **Texan sovereignty:** "If Texas were any bigger, it'd need its own foreign policy."

## 13. Caribbean (Jamaican, Trinidadian, Bajan)

**Comedy DNA:** Anansi trickster tradition, rhythm over grammar, food as cosmic measure, post-colonial compression.
**Vocabulary:** steups (teeth-sucking), wuh loss!, cheese-on-bread!, irie, nuh, cyan, tun roun
**Settings:** the yard, the market, the river crossing, the rum shop, the beach
**Props:** plantain, coco (taro), green banana, the crapaud (frog), the fishing net

**Key Patterns:**
- **Escalation truth:** "Di higher di monkey climb, di more him expose."
- **Double-negation wisdom:** "Nuh call alligator long mout til yuh cross di river."
- **Impossibility marker:** "Crapaud smoke yuh pipe." (the frog is running your life — you're in deep trouble)
- **Satiation contempt:** "When puss belly full, him sey rat taste bitter."
- **Bajan food oath:** "Cheese-on-bread!" (food item as exclamation of shock)
- **Incremental accumulation:** "One one coco full basket."

## 14. Russian

**Comedy DNA:** Fatalism as comedy, finding freedom in having nothing left to lose, wolves as life's constant companion.
**Vocabulary:** nichevo (it's nothing), avos' (maybe/God willing), toska (untranslatable melancholy)
**Settings:** the forest, the izba (wooden house), the kitchen table, the banya (bathhouse), the steppe
**Props:** vodka, the samovar, wolves, bears, birch trees, the barn

**Key Patterns:**
- **Disaster escalation:** "The barn burned — let the house burn too."
- **Tautological comfort:** "If we're alive, we won't be dead." (circular reassurance)
- **Procrastination philosophy:** "Work is not a wolf — it won't run into the forest." (it'll still be there)
- **Nature overrides nurture:** "Feed the wolf all you want, he still eyes the forest."
- **Escalating consumption poetry:** "First glass: a stake. Second: a falcon. The rest: small birds."
- **Death as motivator:** "Can't die twice, but one death is unavoidable."

## 15. Spanish / Latin American

**Comedy DNA:** Animal vulnerability traps, food as moral currency, fatalism with warmth, compressed verdicts.
**Vocabulary:** mañana, no hay mal que por bien no venga, caiga quien caiga, el que no llora no mama
**Settings:** the mercado, the plaza, the cocina, the campo, the cantina
**Props:** tamales, plantains, gumbo (Caribbean), the tortilla, café con leche

**Key Patterns:**
- **Shrimp vulnerability:** "Camarón que se duerme, se lo lleva la corriente." (the shrimp that falls asleep gets carried away)
- **Immutable nature:** "Aunque la mona se vista de seda, mona se queda." (even if the monkey dresses in silk, it's still a monkey)
- **Age vs. species wisdom:** "El burro sabe más por viejo que por burro." (the donkey knows more from being old than from being a donkey)
- **Compressed verdict:** "No da una." (he can't land a single one — 3-word total dismissal)
- **Behavioral contamination:** "El que con lobos anda, a aullar se enseña." (run with wolves, learn to howl)

## 16. German

**Comedy DNA:** Orderliness as moral virtue, compound-word absurdism, blunt assessments, Schadenfreude unashamed.
**Vocabulary:** Ordnung muss sein, das ist mir Wurst (that is sausage to me = I don't care), Verschlimmbessern, Sitzfleisch
**Settings:** the Biergarten, the office, the Autobahn, the recycling station, the Bäckerei
**Props:** sausage, beer, the compound word itself, a well-organized filing system

**Key Patterns:**
- **Compound-word as entire philosophy:** Verschlimmbessern (making things worse while trying to improve them)
- **Sausage indifference:** "Das ist mir Wurst." (that is sausage to me = total equanimity)
- **Unashamed Schadenfreude:** "Schadenfreude ist die schönste Freude." (Schadenfreude is the purest joy)
- **Efficiency as morality:** "Wer zwei Hasen auf einmal jagt, fängt keinen." (chase two hares, catch neither)
- **Backside as moral organ:** Sitzfleisch — the virtue of being able to sit through anything

## 17. Italian

**Comedy DNA:** Life as drama requiring performance, food as both pleasure and philosophy, family as force of nature.
**Vocabulary:** mamma mia, basta, che casino, mangia, non tutte le ciambelle...
**Settings:** the tavola (dining table), the piazza, the mercato, nonna's kitchen, the gesticulating intersection
**Props:** pasta, doughnuts (ciambelle), fish, bread, the second helping, espresso

**Key Patterns:**
- **Table as timeless zone:** "A tavola non si invecchia." (at the table, one does not grow old)
- **Doughnut philosophy:** "Non tutte le ciambelle riescono col buco." (not all doughnuts come out with a hole)
- **Fish hospitality limit:** "The guest is like fish: after three days, it smells."
- **Parallel despair:** "Those who have teeth have no bread; those who have bread have no teeth."
- **Blood over reason:** "Il sangue non è acqua." (blood is not water — family trumps everything)

## 18. French

**Key Patterns:**

## 19. Middle Eastern (Arabic, Persian, Turkish)

**Comedy DNA:** Animal's defining feature becomes the joke, desert survival as moral calibration, small ceremonies hide enormous social contracts.
**Vocabulary:** maktub (it is written), inshallah, yalla, habibi, çay (tea)
**Settings:** the bazaar, the desert, the tea house, the oasis, the caravanserai
**Props:** camels, the sieve, tea/coffee, the carpet, dates, the scale

**Key Patterns:**
- **Camel self-blindness:** "A camel cannot see its own hump." (can't see your own defining flaw)
- **Pursuit-loss paradox:** "The camel went looking for horns and lost its ears." (greed costs you what you had)
- **Persian extra beat:** "The fox said the grapes are sour — but he stretched his neck all the same." (the body betrays the lie)
- **Coffee as contract:** "A cup of coffee commits one to forty years of friendship." (Turkish)
- **Sea of accumulated grief:** "They asked the sea: 'Why are you salty?' It said: 'Ask the rivers that have wept into me.'" (Persian)
- **Asymmetric vigilance:** "The buyer needs a hundred eyes; the seller needs only one." (Persian bazaar)

## 20. East Asian (Japanese, Chinese, Korean)

**Comedy DNA:** Four-character compression (Chinese), seasonal precision (Japanese), untranslatable puns (Korean), conformity commentary.

### Japanese
**Vocabulary:** shikata ga nai (it can't be helped), mottainai (what a waste), ganbatte
**Key Patterns:**
- **Expert failure:** "Even monkeys fall from trees." (saru mo ki kara ochiru)
- **Wasted on the unworthy:** "Gold coins to a cat." (neko ni koban)
- **Nail conformity:** "The nail that sticks up gets hammered down." (deru kui wa utareru)
- **Counterintuitive speed:** "If in a hurry, take the long way around." (isogaba maware)

### Chinese
**Key Patterns:**
- **Ruined by overdoing:** "Drawing a snake, then adding feet." (画蛇添足)
- **Refined art for the unappreciative:** "Playing the qin to a cow." (对牛弹琴)
- **Self-deception:** "Covering your ears while stealing a bell." (掩耳盗铃)
- **Borrowed authority:** "A fox borrows the tiger's fierceness." (狐假虎威)
- **Repeated lies become truth:** "Three men make a tiger." (三人成虎)

### Korean
**Key Patterns:**
- **Food envy:** "Other people's rice cakes look bigger." (남의 떡이 더 커 보인다)
- **Premature celebration:** "Don't drink the kimchi soup before getting the rice cake."
- **24-hour surveillance:** "Birds hear daytime words; rats hear nighttime words."
- **Words-as-horse pun:** "Words without feet travel a thousand li." (발 = feet, 말 = words/horse)

## 21. Indian / South Asian

**Comedy DNA:** Animal hierarchy as social commentary, food and spice as moral currency, Bollywood as shared reference, jugaad (resourceful workaround) as philosophy.
**Vocabulary:** jugaad, chalta hai (it'll do), namak haram (salt traitor), arre yaar, accha
**Settings:** the chai stall, the bazaar, nani/dadi's kitchen, the auto-rickshaw, the wedding
**Props:** chai, mangoes, ginger, dal, the mustache (as character indicator), rice cakes

**Key Patterns:**
- **Monkey-ginger dismissal:** "What does a monkey know of the taste of ginger?" (bandar kya jaane adrak ka swaad)
- **Elephant hypocrisy:** "An elephant has different teeth for eating and for showing."
- **Crow-swan imitation collapse:** "The crow tried to walk like a swan and forgot its own walk."
- **Premature mustache oil:** "Jackfruit still on the tree, but oil already on the mustache." (Bengali)
- **Status deadlock:** "You're a queen, I'm a queen — who fetches the water?" (Punjabi)
- **Fancy gap:** "Fancy shop, bland dish." (oonchi dukaan, pheeka pakwaan)

## 22. African (Igbo, Akan, Yoruba, Swahili, Zulu)

**Comedy DNA:** Trickster outsmarts himself at peak cleverness, animals as full protagonists with agency, sound pattern teaches before meaning lands.
**Vocabulary:** ubuntu, haraka haraka haina baraka, tembo (elephant), Anansi
**Settings:** the village, the market, the forest, the river, the elder's compound, under the baobab
**Props:** the kola nut, the yam, the pot of wisdom, the drum, the hump

**Key Patterns:**
- **Hyena self-justification:** "When the hyena wants to eat its children, it first accuses them of smelling like goats."
- **Elephant memory (3 words):** "Tembo hukumbuka." (The elephant remembers.)
- **Rhyming wisdom:** "Haraka haraka haina baraka." (hurry hurry has no blessing — Swahili)
- **Chief without subjects:** "A chief with no subjects is simply a man standing in a field."
- **Trickster overreach:** Anansi tried to keep all wisdom in a pot — his son saw wisdom is too big for one pot.
- **Active patience:** "Subira huvuta kheri." (patience draws blessings to itself — Swahili)
- **Ubuntu:** "Umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu." (a person is a person through other persons)
